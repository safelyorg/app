FAQ

Common questions.

Do you offer a free trial?

Yes — every paid plan starts with a free trial and no credit card required. Pro and Business include a 7-day trial, and the API plan includes 14 days, so you can test full real-world protection before paying anything.

Which marketplaces does Safely support?

Safely works on any website where buying and selling happens — Ebay, Facebook Marketplace, Amazon, Vingo, Alibaba, and more. The extension is platform-agnostic: if there is a listing, Safely can analyze it.

How does the AI analyze listings?

When you open a listing, Safely reads the title, description, price, seller name, account age, location, and images from the page. This data is sent to our backend which uses Claude AI to analyze 8 distinct fraud signals and return a risk score from 0–100 with a detailed breakdown of every finding.

Who sees my fraud reports?

Fraud reports are shared across the Safely community — they are visible to any user who views the same seller. Your identity is never attached to a report. Reports only show the reason and timestamp.

Does Safely slow my browser down?

No. The extension only activates on supported marketplace pages and runs analysis in the background without blocking page load. Analysis typically completes within 2 seconds and has no noticeable impact on browser performance.

Is my browsing data private?

We only collect listing and seller data from pages you actively visit on supported marketplaces. We never track your browsing history, never sell your data to third parties, and all analysis data is stored securely. You can delete your account and all associated data at any time.

Which specific pages does Safely actually scan?

Right now, Safely actively reads listing details on OLX. Facebook Marketplace support is in progress and being rolled out. Safely only activates on individual listing pages — simply browsing a marketplace's homepage, your own profile, or any other page on a supported site won't trigger an analysis. Support for more marketplaces, including eBay, Craigslist, Gumtree, and Vinted, is on our roadmap.

Safely vs Norton

Norton and similar antivirus tools protect your computer — they catch malware, viruses, and malicious files trying to attack your device.

Safely protects you from a different kind of attack entirely: a real person on the other end of a listing, manipulating your judgement into sending money for something that doesn't exist. No file is downloaded, no malware is involved — which is exactly why antivirus software has nothing to detect. Safely and antivirus solve two different problems, and most people benefit from both.

Got it

Safely vs manual research

Most buyers today check a listing by reading it carefully, searching the seller's name, and going with their gut. That works reasonably well for obvious scams, but takes several minutes per listing and still misses the subtler signals — recycled images, slightly-off pricing, or urgency language designed specifically to bypass exactly that kind of gut check.

Safely runs the same checks a careful buyer would want to do, automatically, in about 2 seconds, every time you open a listing.

Got it

Safely vs basic seller reviews

Star ratings and reviews built into marketplaces are useful, but a brand-new seller account naturally has none yet — which tells you nothing either way, and is exactly the situation most scam listings are posted from.

Safely doesn't rely on a review history existing at all. It analyzes the listing itself — the language used, the images, the price, the seller's account age — so it can flag risk even on a seller's very first post.

Got it

Safely vs generic browser extensions

Ad blockers, price trackers, and coupon extensions are built to change or add something to a page — they were never designed to evaluate whether the person selling to you is trustworthy.

Safely is purpose-built around one question: is this specific listing and seller safe to send money to. That's the only thing it does, which is why it can go deep on it — 8 distinct fraud signals, seller history, and a community fraud report network, all in one focused tool.
